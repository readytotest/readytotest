# Welcome to My QA Laboratory! 🥼🧪🔬

## About Me

At the core I'm an **exploratory tester** with roots in **UX research** and **usability testing**. Stacked on top of that is **test automation** and **CI/CD**. I learn by doing, and **I broke things a hundred different ways before figuring out how to get everything working**.  

I don't just skim things or assume things are solid just because they look solid. **I pick it up, shake it, and see what falls out**. **I support the team by spotting problems before they become bigger headaches**. I'm not just walking the "happy path." I'm making sure the product holds up where it really counts.

All my repos revolve around **automated testing**, and **CI/CD**. My focus has shifted primarily to [Playwright/Typescript](https://github.com/readytotest/test-playwright), with an entire repo dedicated to it. Early on I started with [Cypress/JavaScript](https://readytotest.github.io/html/cypress.htm), but those projects have no helpers and more rigid functions. At my current job, a developer taught me how to write more flexible functions in Playwright. I've learned a lot from working closely with developers. Every PR or website update triggers automated tests and publishes results to a [GitHub Pages report dashboard](https://readytotest.github.io/playwright-allure-report/). See the YAML workflow setup [here](https://github.com/readytotest/readytotest.github.io/blob/main/.github/workflows/playwright-mysite.yml).  

I also get into other things, like building [The Repo Clean-O-Matic](https://github.com/readytotest/playwright-allure-report/blob/main/.github/workflows/repo-clean-o-matic.yml), a YAML config that deletes files on a cron schedule to keep my report repo size from getting out of hand.

## My QA Philosophy in a Nutshell 🥜
### Don't just make sure nothing's on fire, ask why the gas line's leaking. 💥  

**QA exists to protect the business and be an advocate for the end user**  
It's about spotting risks before they turn into bigger problems: [gaps in requirements](https://readytotest.github.io/html/requirement-gap.htm), friction in the UX, security holes, compliance issues. I'm not just verifying what the team does know. I'm poking around for what no one thought to question. The unidentified risks. That's where the real danger hides.

**QA is not just clicking buttons and marking things pass/fail** or trusting specs blindly, especially when things feel off. It's about digging deeper, getting involved early, questioning assumptions, and asking whether things actually make sense. You're not just testing the features; you’re testing the whole experience. As QA, you help your team fix those gas leaks before they become fires.

  >✨ QA shouldn't only be the person who shows up after the party and says, "**Hey, someone spilled wine on the couch.**" 🍷 QA should be the one saying, "**Hey, maybe don't set the red wine next to the dance floor in the first place.**" 
  
### Automation helps, but it doesn’t replace exploratory testing  
Human intuition and creativity catch what scripts miss.
